How Binaural Focus Sessions Work

Binaural beats are created when each ear hears a slightly different tone. Your brain processes the difference as a rhythmic beat sensation. In practice, many people use this method to maintain attention during study, coding, writing, and deep work.

LIDU lets you combine this with rain, wind, ocean, or cafe ambience, so your focus environment feels stable and less fatiguing over longer sessions.

Suggested Setup for Deep Work

  • Start with Binaural Focus mode and keep intensity at a moderate level.
  • Add one ambient texture to mask distracting sounds around you.
  • Run a 25-minute focus block, then take a short reset break.
  • Repeat for 2 to 4 cycles, depending on your energy level.

When to Use Binaural Relax Instead

If your mind feels overloaded or anxious, switch to Binaural Relax for a lower-pressure session. You can then transition back to focus mode when your attention stabilizes.

FAQ

Do binaural beats actually help focus?

Many people report better concentration when binaural beats are paired with clear goals, a timer, and fewer notifications.

What is the best session length?

Start with 25 minutes and adjust after a week of use. Short, repeatable sessions usually perform better than long, inconsistent ones.
